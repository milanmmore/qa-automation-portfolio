My QA Journey

“If I could do it, so can you.”

From a non-IT background to leading QA teams—here’s how I built a fulfilling career in software testing and automation.

📍 Where It Started

My academic journey began with a strong foundation in science. I chose it as my career path and completed my graduation with determination and curiosity. My parents—especially my mother—always encouraged me to pursue education and become a successful woman. Her belief in me inspired me to aim higher, and I enrolled in a Master’s program in Inorganic Chemistry.

"Education is the most powerful gift you can give yourself. Keep learning, keep rising." – My Mother

After completing my post-graduation, I moved to Pune following my marriage. I was eager to start working, but most chemical companies were located far outside the city, making it difficult to find a suitable opportunity nearby.

With my husband’s support, I decided to pivot. I enrolled in a computer course and later pursued an MBA, opening the door to a new world of possibilities. It was the Y2K era—a time of transformation in the tech industry—and I landed my first job in an IT company as a software developer.

Back then, developers were responsible for testing their own code. When the QA role emerged, I saw an opportunity to shift into testing. The demanding schedule of development pushed me toward QA, and I found myself truly enjoying every phase of the journey —from learning the basics to leading teams and mentoring others. Testing gave me not just a career, but a purpose.

“Sometimes the best paths are the ones we never planned. Testing found me—and I found myself in it.”

🚀 Growth & Mastery

My passion for software testing grew stronger with every challenge I faced. I eventually enrolled in an automation course to learn Selenium, and with the help of Google and real-time projects, I began applying it practically. I also taught myself Java—despite not coming from a computer science background—which was no small feat.

Today, I’m exploring Generative AI and its potential to transform QA workflows. But the journey hasn’t always been smooth. The toughest part was interviews—especially when I lacked hands-on experience. I realized that without practical knowledge, it’s hard to express your capabilities confidently.

That’s why I created this platform: to bring together all the tools, resources, and insights I wish I had when I started. If I struggled with it, chances are someone else is too—and I want to make their path easier.

There’s still so much more to learn. The world is evolving, technologies are changing—but I believe where there’s a will, there’s a way. We all start somewhere. And if you’re on a similar ground, know that you’re not alone.

“You don’t need a tech degree to thrive in QA—you need curiosity, courage, and consistency.”

💡 What Drives Me

After completing a long-term project, I was ready to transition to a new challenge within my organization. The interview—conducted by an internal employee—was for a role involving a new tool and a different programming language. Though I had given a client interview four years earlier, this felt like stepping into unfamiliar territory.

I embraced the opportunity to learn, dedicating myself to mastering the new tool. My first interview came just one month into this journey, followed by another two months later. Despite my experience and readiness for the role, the interviews focused heavily on coding syntax—an area that often overshadows practical capability.

That experience was eye-opening. It reminded me that even seasoned professionals can face hurdles—not due to lack of skill, but because interview formats don’t always reflect real-world QA responsibilities.

I decided to turn this challenge into a mission: to share my journey, consolidate my learnings, and create resources that help others navigate similar paths. This platform is my way of giving back—empowering testers with practical knowledge, confidence, and community.

“Interviews test syntax. Experience proves capability.”
“Every challenge is a chance to grow—and to help others grow with you.”

In reality, every tester leans on existing automation frameworks, community support, and now, Generative AI. I’ve rarely seen a tester write flawless code from memory—but I’ve seen many solve real-world problems with collaboration and creativity.

That’s why I believe it’s time to shift from traditional interview techniques to a modern, practical approach. I started learning automation tools with the help of Gen AI, and now I’m committed to helping others do the same—especially those who feel overwhelmed by the gap between theory and practice.

I believe in sharing knowledge and helping others grow. That’s why I created this site—to guide testers through the challenges I once faced. Whether you're just starting or looking to level up, I’m here to help you learn faster and grow confidently.

“Real testers don’t memorize—they strategize.”
“Knowledge grows when shared. That’s how communities thrive.”
“You don’t need to know everything. You just need to know how to learn.”
“The best testers aren’t just tool experts—they’re problem solvers, mentors, and lifelong learners.”
